A Short History

The first graphical browsers appeared in the early 1990s (e.g., Mosaic), followed by Netscape Navigator. With Internet Explorer’s release (1995), vendor competition spurred rapid releases—and fragmentation—through proprietary tags and scripting quirks that complicated standards work.

In the early 2000s, Mozilla/Firefox championed tabbed browsing and extensibility, eroding IE’s dominance. September 2008 saw the launch of Google Chrome, reframing expectations for speed, sandboxing, and auto-updates.

Under the hood, engines evolved: Apple forked KHTML into WebKit (Safari). Google forked WebKit into Blink for Chrome; Blink now powers Chrome, Edge, Opera, Brave, and others. In 2018, Microsoft moved Edge to Chromium (Blink + V8) for compatibility, consolidating engines into Blink, WebKit, and Gecko.
