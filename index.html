Browsers: Past, Present, and Possible Futures

A browser is a user’s window to the internet. It translates open standards into pages and apps, enforces security boundaries, and increasingly acts as a runtime for applications. Because of this centrality, browser competition, design, and governance have outsized impact on users, developers, publishers, and regulators.
