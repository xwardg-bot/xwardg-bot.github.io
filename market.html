The State of the Market

As of August 2025, Chrome accounts for around 69% of global usage, followed by Safari (~15%), Edge (~5%), and Firefox (~2–3%). North America has a higher Safari share due to iOS. A concentration at the top means a small set of vendors heavily influences the web’s trajectory.

Engine Monoculture Concerns

  • Sites are often built primarily against Chromium/Blink.
  • Diversity across engines improves standards quality and web health.
  • Independent implementations (e.g., Gecko) provide checks on platform direction.

Mozilla continues to innovate on privacy and standards incubation, remaining a viable independent engine despite smaller share.
